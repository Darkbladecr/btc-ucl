01. Research 

Firstly, we endeavor into the hospital environment and just observe the daily interactions of the general public with the various healthcare professionals. We assess the intricacies of each contact and what can be done to improve the speed, efficacy and delivery of care.

./wp-content/themes/salient/css/fonts/svg/basic_todo_txt.svg
 

02. Record

Secondly, we record these interactions and quantitatively power our observations with data. We may discover new ways to change care and improve the quality of our healthcare system. We involve both the healthcare professionals and the patients to provide input in this stage and provide further insight that may not be apparent from the observer’s role.

./wp-content/themes/salient/css/fonts/svg/ecommerce_graph_increase.svg

03. Improve

Thirdly, we critically appraise the current system and take the patient’s perspective to understand the areas of improvement. We use technology to analyze our baseline data and through creative thinking apply change concepts to the current system. Recording is continued through these interventions to see the effect of our changes in real-time.

~Stefan Mitrasinovic

Simple Ideas… Big Impact!

What is the difference between Quality Improvement and Research?

Quality Improvement (QI) projects use evidence-based medicine in an innovative way, changing practice in small specific measurable and simple ways. Medical innovation and change is typically very slow. The way in which QI projects run means that they are fast and sensitive to the ongoing collection of data. So changes can be seen in real-time and implemented quickly.
